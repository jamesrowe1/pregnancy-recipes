Steak Fajitas with Peppers & Onions

Grill, Pregnancy Super Food, Stovetop

Servings: 4 servings Source: Babycenter.com

Ingredients

1 flank steak (about 1 and 1/3 lb/655 g)

2 avocados

1 clove garlic, minced

Juice of 1/2 lime

2 tablespoons olive oil

2 small red bell peppers, seeded and thickly sliced

2 small yellow onions, thickly sliced

8 large tortillas, warmed


For the marinade:

1/4 cup (2 fl oz/60 ml) fresh lime juice

1/4 cup (1/3 oz/10 g) chopped fresh cilantro

1 tablespoon olive oil

1 teaspoon minced jalapeño pepper

1 teaspoon minced garlic

1/2 teaspoon ground cumin

Salt and freshly ground pepper

Directions

To make the marinade, in a zippered plastic bag or nonreactive bowl, mix the lime juice, cilantro, olive oil, jalapeño, garlic, cumin, and salt and pepper to taste. Add the steak and massage or turn to coat. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour or up to overnight.

Halve and pit the avocados, and scoop the flesh into a bowl. Add the garlic and lime juice and mash with a fork until almost smooth. Cover with plastic wrap, pressing it directly onto the surface, and set aside.

Preheat a charcoal or gas grill to medium-high heat. While the grill is preheating, make the vegetables: In a frying pan over medium-high heat, warm 1 tablespoon of the olive oil. Add the bell peppers and sauté until just tender and lightly browned on the edges, 3 to 5 minutes. Transfer to a serving dish and set aside. Return the pan to the heat and add the remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil. Add the onion and sauté until just tender and lightly browned on the edges, 3 to 5 minutes. Transfer to the serving dish holding the peppers. Cover and keep warm until ready to serve.

Remove the steak from the marinade and discard the marinade. Place the steak over the hottest part of the grill and cook, turning once, until the internal temperature reaches 145° F (63° C) and done to your liking, about 5 minutes per side for medium rare. Transfer the steak to a cutting board and let rest for 3-5 minutes. Slice the steak thinly across the grain.

Place the steak, peppers and onions, tortillas, and mashed avocado on the table for diners to serve themselves. To make a fajita, take a tortilla, dollop with a spoonful of the mashed avocado, and top with some of the peppers, onions, and steak. Serve warm.
